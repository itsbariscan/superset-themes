# Atlas — moodboard

**Concept:** An old map atlas, opened on a library table. Faded teal water, dust-rose place names, mountain-shadow brown, parchment ground.

**Anchor references:** WPA-era topographic maps, the National Geographic Atlas of the World (1963 ed.), Stanford's Rumsey Map Collection. Specifically the *muted, ink-printed* maps before glossy CMYK took over.

**Anchor colors:**
- Parchment ground: `#ebe2cb` (slightly darker than Vellum's cream)
- Foreground: `#2a2620` (warm dark brown ink — never black)
- Faded teal: `#3e7a78` (primary — water, rivers, coastline)
- Place-name red: `#a8473a` (destructive — capitals, borders)
- Map border: `#b3a684` (deck edges)
- Mountain shadow: `#6a5e48` (mutedForeground)
- Highway gold: `#c89e4f` (highlight)

**Mood:** Light theme. Distinct from Vellum (sepia/single-accent) and Inkwell (blue/literary): Atlas is multi-color but all *muted*, like ink that has aged uniformly. The five chart colors are the four-color cartographic palette plus a forest green.

**Type:** light

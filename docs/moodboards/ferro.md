# Ferro — moodboard

**Concept:** A blacksmith's forge at first light. Cold dark steel meets hot orange embers. The metal hasn't lost its memory of fire.

**Anchor references:** A coal forge, an anvil, oxidized scale on hammered steel, the moment a piece comes out of the quench. Tom Joyce blacksmithing, the Hephaestus aesthetic.

**Anchor colors:**
- Cold steel: `#15181c` (gun-blue dark, with cool cast)
- Foreground: `#dcd4c8` (warm bone — the off-white of cooled metal)
- Forge orange: `#e85d1e` (the heart of an open coal — primary)
- Ember red: `#c63a1c` (destructive — older fire)
- Quench black: `#0c0d10` (sidebar — the tank)
- Anvil grey: `#3a3d44` (border)
- Spark yellow: `#f5b042` (highlight — flash of hot work)

**Mood:** Industrial, but elemental — Ferro vs. Hangar: Ferro is fire and metal, Hangar is concrete and process. Ferro's primary is warmer, with a deeper edge.

**Type:** dark

# Kintsugi — moodboard

**Concept:** Black lacquer bowl mended with gold dust. Cracks showing where the break was. The philosophy: the repair is the point, not the imperfection to hide.

**Anchor references:** Kintsugi pottery, Japanese tea-ceremony lacquerware, Mariko Kusumoto's gold-thread textile work. Also: Dieter Rams's "less but better" — but with poetry.

**Anchor colors:**
- Lacquer black: `#0d0a08` (background — warm-cast deep black)
- Foreground: `#e6dac8` (cream — a slightly aged white)
- Kintsugi gold: `#d4a04a` (primary — the seam)
- Mended-cream: `#e6dac8` (highlight foreground)
- Gold-leaf glow: `#f0c878` (highlightActive)
- Crack-shadow brown: `#1a1612` (card)
- Vermillion red: `#c14747` (destructive — single hot accent)
- Border: `#2a221a`

**Mood:** Dark, restrained, slightly luxurious — Velvet Rope was considered, but Kintsugi has a clearer concept and is less hospitality-cliché. The gold should be the *only* warm note. Greens absent.

**Type:** dark
